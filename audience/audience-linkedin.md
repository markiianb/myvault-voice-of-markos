# Audience: Markos on LinkedIn

Markos does not visualise a single reader. His mental model is his existing network — roughly 3,000 followers at the time of the voice session — drawn from a 25-year enterprise software career. He wants **wide reach, not narrow AI-developer reach.**

*"All I'm saying is for us to get a wide, kind of wide reach, I think, you know, we can try to go quite wide. Not just about AI models."* — Markos, 2026-04-10.

## The five segments (his framing)

1. **Ex-colleagues from enterprise software.** CIOs, CXOs, CISOs, IT security officers, heads of tech. People he's hired, sold systems to, dealt with across his career. *"All these sorts of people that I've engaged with, hired, dealt with, sold systems to in the past."*

2. **Vendor-side peers.** Sales engineering, product management, and management inside tech vendors. *"People that work for vendors, typically in either sales engineering or management, product management, things like that."*

3. **Consultants in adjacency.** AI and tech consultants, ex-ITSM peers now in advisory roles. *"People that bird my network that also provide consulting in these areas too."*

4. **Personal-life buyers.** Same professional network wearing their consumer hat: high-income, management-level, privacy-conscious families. *"They might be in corporate, but in their personal lives they're also quite good candidates… quite high income and management."*

5. **NOT developers.** Explicitly out. *"There's a certain amount of people that just, that's what they care about — the latest clicky downloadable plugin. They're probably mainly like developers, to be honest. And they're probably not our target market."*

## Cross-industry breadth (his claim to reach)

Markos's career spans retail, logistics, manufacturing, banking, services, utility, energy, defence, and government. Content that draws on any of these lands with Segment 1 and Segment 3. *"I understand how a retail organization works, how a logistics organization works, how a manufacturing organization works, how bank works, whatever services organization — a utility, an energy company, a defense department, a government department."*

## Audience-to-content matching

| Content pillar | Lands with |
|---|---|
| Architecture, pragmatic AI delivery, "why IT projects fail" | Segments 1 (CIOs) and 3 (consultants) |
| Big-tech critique, data brokers, open-source AI | Segments 1, 2, and 4 |
| Education, democratising AI, free certification idea | Segment 4 primarily; also 3 |
| Accountability, consumer data rights, platform liability | Segment 4 primarily; also 1 on governance angle |
| Privacy-as-control, Zero Trust, data portability | All segments, with Segment 4 weighted heaviest |

## What does NOT land

- Plugin-of-the-week tooling chatter.
- Hot-takes on Twitter drama, celebrity-CEO gossip, politics.
- Generic AI hype ("AI is changing everything").
- Binary two-sentence opinion posts that require a side.
- Anything that reads as preaching to Segment 1 (they have more scars than Markos; they see through it).

Write for the shared interest across segments: **pragmatic technologists who have been burned by hype, who want new angles, and who still trust expertise.** When a topic only lands with one segment, that's fine — just pick the segment deliberately.
