# Newsjack Craft

Newsjacks are high-reach but high-risk for Markos's boundaries. They are where preach-register creeps in. Use this chunk every time.

## The newsjack arc (5 parts, compressed)

1. **The news in one line.** Don't re-report; reference. "Google just [thing]." Or: "A new study says [thing]."
2. **What most people will take from it.** The obvious read. One line.
3. **What Markos thinks they miss.** The pragmatic angle, the structural angle, the pattern — not a hot take.
4. **Evidence or experience.** A specific story, number, or structural observation in 1–2 sentences.
5. **The implication.** What this means for a practitioner or decision-maker. Not an exhortation — an observation.

## The preach test

Newsjacks fail here most often. Before publishing, check:

- No "everyone should" / "wake up" / "people need to."
- No exclamation marks.
- No binary right/wrong declaratives.
- Does the post leave the reader with agency — or does it tell them what to do?

If any of the above trips, rewrite. If the post still reads preachy after two rewrites, spike it.

## Tone

Measured. Observational. Wry when it's big-tech-behaviour-obvious ("Classic move."). Never hostile.

## Topics that fit newsjacks *(Green Zone per `opinions/opinions.md`)*

- Big-tech data breaches or policy changes
- AI governance news (EU AI Act rulings, US executive orders)
- Open-source AI releases (Qwen, Deep Seek, Llama)
- AI-in-education stories
- Consumer-data-rights rulings
- Subscription / dark-pattern regulation

## Topics that are NEVER newsjacks

- Politics, elections, partisan policy
- Celebrity-CEO drama
- AI doomerism / existential risk hot-takes
- Anything requiring a binary right/wrong position
- Anything touching named big-tech employees personally

## Turnaround

- **Ideal:** within 6 hours of the news landing in his audience's feed.
- **Hard deadline:** within 24 hours, or skip the cycle.
- **Approval:** for time-sensitive newsjacks, draft → schedule → Slack Markos → publish on approval. If no response in 4 hours, hold.

## Length

100–250 words. Shorter beats longer for newsjacks. One idea, sharply made.

## Frequency

One per week, maximum. Newsjacks lose power when they become a regular format — they should feel like Markos happened to notice something, not like the account chases news cycles.
