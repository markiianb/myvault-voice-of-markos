# Target Accounts

The accounts Sead engages with from Markos's account come from six categories. Markos approves the named list before we engage. We review monthly.

## Category framework

| Category | What these accounts are | Why we engage | Volume |
|----------|------------------------|---------------|--------|
| **Ex-colleagues — enterprise software** | CIOs, CXOs, CISOs, IT security officers from Markos's network (ITSM / asset / service management era) | Peer network, they're now in AI-adjacent roles, mutual amplification | 8–10 accounts |
| **Vendor-side peers** | Sales engineering, PM, management at AI/security/infra vendors | Industry amplification, cross-company visibility | 5–7 accounts |
| **Consultants in adjacency** | Ex-ITSM consultants, now in AI/tech advisory | Word-of-mouth into Fortune 1000s, blog post distribution | 4–6 accounts |
| **AI/privacy thought leaders** | Researchers, journalists, advocates in AI privacy and open-source space | Category positioning, potential media coverage | 3–5 accounts |
| **Open-source AI voices** | People posting about Qwen, Deep Seek, Llama, sovereign AI | Aligns with his open-source pillar | 3–5 accounts |
| **Privacy-conscious founders / operators** | Founders building privacy-adjacent products | Peer alliance, mutual promotion | 3–5 accounts |

**Total starting list: 25–35 accounts.** Markos reviews and approves before we engage. Add/remove monthly based on engagement quality.

## Engagement quality markers

Markos cares about engagement quality over volume. When we engage, we want accounts that:

- Post substantive content (not hot takes)
- Have engaged audiences (5%+ engagement rate on average post)
- Are in Markos's credibility graph (he'd be happy to be associated publicly)
- Reciprocate engagement (conversations, not broadcast)

An account with 2,000 engaged followers in CIO-land beats an account with 200,000 passive followers in general tech. Weight accordingly.

## Review cadence

- Monthly review of the full list
- Add/remove 2–3 accounts per month
- Track engagement quality — are comments driving conversation?
- Drop accounts that go silent (no reciprocation over 60 days)
- Flag any account whose content drifts into Red Zone topics
